Positiones Nominalium

English CIMAGL 61 (1991), edited by Sten Ebbesen Vat. lat. 7678: 88rA
These are our positions concerning universals.

  1. First, we consent that universals, like genera and species, are names (nomina).

  2. Second, we posit, against the opinion of the reales, that nothing exists aside from particulars.

  3. Third, we disagree with what is proposed by Aristotle in the book on Generation, namely that something can grow (augmentari)

  4. Fourth, we say, with Zeno, that it does not appear that something can move, to which Aristotle disagrees at the end of the sixth book of Physics.

  5. We state that there is no action, except via pores (per poros).

  6. Again, we say, with Zeno, that being is said univocally about those things which are (de hiis quae sunt).

  7. Moreover, we say that there are only two universals, namely genus and species.

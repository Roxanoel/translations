Positiones Nominalium

English CIMAGL 61 (1991), edited by Sten Ebbesen Vat. lat. 7678: 88rA
These are our positions concerning universals.

  1. First, we consent that universals, like genera and species, are names (nomina).

  2. Second, we posit, against the opinion of the reales, that nothing exists aside from particulars.

  3. Third, we disagree with what is proposed by Aristotle in the book on Generation, namely that something can grow (augmentari)

  4. Fourth, we say, with Zeno, that it does not appear that something can move, to which Aristotle disagrees at the end of the sixth book of Physics.

  5. We state that there is no action, except via pores (per poros).

  6. Again, we say, with Zeno, that being is said univocally about those things which are (de hiis quae sunt).

  7. Moreover, we say that there are only two universals, namely genus and species;

  8. and that some accident is in a subject which neither denominates the subject simpliciter, nor according to the "what" (secundum quid);

  9. and that "accident" contains under itself all universals.

  10. Again, we say that grammar, dialectic, and rhetoric are a single science (unam scientiam), against the division of Al-Farabi;

  11. besides this we disagree with what Priscian is saying, namely that a syllab cannot be had in a meter at many times (plura tempora), because we posit that a syllab can be had in an infinite time.

  12. Also we disagree with the same (Priscian) and agree with the Stoics, who posited that an interjection has to be included among adverbs.

  13. Again, we posit that it is insufficient, because he did not teach the species of figures of speech (topoi).

  14. Likewise, we posit that no construction is faulty.

  15. Similarly, against Aristotle, we say that the book On Interpretation is thoroughly superfluous/overabundant.

  16. And again that it is diminished, because it did not thoroughly teach the art of dividing,

  17. and that all science is demonstrative, not topical (of the topics).
